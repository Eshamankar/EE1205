\textbf{Question 11.9.3.11:} 
\begin{enumerate}
\item Evaluate 
\[
\sum_{k=1}^{11} (2 + 3^k)
\]
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Solution: }

The solution of the summation
\[
\sum_{k=1}^{11} (2 + 3^k)
\]
is
\[
x(11) - x(0) = \left[2(12) + \frac{3^{12} - 1}{2}\right] - \left[2(1) + \frac{3^{1} - 1}{2}\right]
\]

Simplifying further:
\[
x(11) - x(0) = 24 + \frac{3^{12} - 1}{2} - \left[2(1) + 1\right]
\]
\[
= 24 + \frac{3^{12} - 1}{2} - 3
\]
\[
= 21+ \frac{3^{12} - 1}{2}
\]
